# October 2025 ICE/CBP Deaths - Research Summary

**Research Date:** February 5, 2026
**Researcher:** Oilcloth
**Assignment:** Document 3 deaths from October 2025

---

## Overview

Three individuals died in October 2025 due to ICE enforcement actions or medical neglect in ICE custody. These deaths represent different patterns of how immigration enforcement kills:

1. **Josué Castro Rivera** - Pursuit death (killed fleeing ICE)
2. **Gabriel Garcia-Aviles** - Medical neglect death (preventable alcohol withdrawal)
3. **Kai Yin Wong** - Medical complications death (heart surgery while in custody)

All three deaths were preventable. None have resulted in accountability.

---

## Case 1: Josué Castro Rivera - The Pursuit Death

**Date:** October 23, 2025
**Location:** Interstate 264, Norfolk, Virginia
**Age:** 24-25
**Nationality:** Honduras

### What Happened

Castro Rivera was traveling to his gardening job when ICE stopped the van he was riding in. During a "targeted, intelligence-based operation," agents attempted to detain him and three other passengers. He fled on foot and attempted to cross Interstate 264, where he was struck and killed by a pickup truck.

### Critical Facts

- **No removal order** - Castro Rivera had no pending deportation order or criminal case
- **Collateral arrest** - He was not the target of the "targeted" operation
- **Foreseeable risk** - ICE created situation where flight led to active highway
- **No accountability** - No federal investigation, unlike shooting deaths
- **4 years in U.S.** - Working, sending money to family in Honduras

### Pattern Recognition

This is a **pursuit death**, where ICE enforcement actions create deadly situations without direct use of force. Key pattern:
1. ICE conducts enforcement operation
2. Subject flees due to fear/immigration status
3. Pursuit occurs near dangerous environment (highway, water, etc.)
4. Death results from environmental hazard
5. ICE attributes death to "fleeing" rather than acknowledging their role
6. No accountability

### Family Response

Brother Henry Castro: "He didn't deserve everything that happened to him. He had a very good heart." Called death "an injustice."

Family raising funds to repatriate body to Honduras. GoFundMe exceeded $10,000 goal.

### Advocacy Response

**CASA Virginia (Eduardo Zelaya):**
> "Josué's death is the result of weak leadership in Virginia that has allowed fear to become part of everyday life by letting ICE terrorize immigrant families."

Demands: Full investigation, accountability for ICE actions, review of pursuit policies.

### Investigations

- **Virginia State Police:** Investigating traffic fatality, no charges against driver
- **Federal:** NO investigation announced (contrast with shooting deaths like Alex Pretti)
- **Accountability:** NONE

### Why This Matters

- Demonstrates ICE kills without direct force
- "Targeted" operations routinely sweep up non-targets
- Pursuit deaths get less scrutiny than shootings
- No investigation means pattern will continue

## Case 2: Gabriel Garcia-Aviles - The Medical Neglect Death

**Date:** October 23, 2025 (same day as Castro Rivera)
**Location:** Victor Valley Global Medical Center, Victorville, California (from Adelanto ICE Processing Center)
**Age:** 54-56 (sources vary)
**Nationality:** Mexico

### What Happened

Garcia-Aviles was arrested by ICE on October 15, 2025 and transferred to Adelanto ICE Processing Center. Upon arrival, he immediately exhibited severe alcohol withdrawal symptoms (disorientation, confusion, impaired mobility). He was hospitalized the same day but his condition worsened. **Family was not notified for 8 days.** When his daughter finally found him on October 23, he was unconscious, intubated, with dried blood on his forehead. Family requested withdrawal of life support. He died from cardiac arrest due to alcohol withdrawal syndrome.

### Critical Facts

- **30+ years in U.S.** - Long-term resident with established life in Costa Mesa, CA
- **Preventable death** - Alcohol withdrawal syndrome is treatable with proper medical care
- **Family not notified for 8 days** - Denied opportunity to advocate during critical period
- **Physical trauma** - Dried blood on forehead suggests injuries beyond medical neglect
- **Second death at Adelanto in one month** - Ismael Ayala-Uribe died September 22
- **Congressional investigation** - 43 representatives signed letter accusing ICE of "gross negligence"

### Pattern Recognition

This is a **medical neglect death**, where ICE fails to provide adequate medical screening and care. Key pattern:
1. ICE arrests person with medical condition
2. Inadequate or no medical screening before/during arrest
3. Detention facility fails to provide proper care
4. Condition deteriorates to emergency
5. Hospital transfer when crisis can't be ignored
6. Death after delayed intervention
7. ICE attributes to "pre-existing condition"

### Congressional Response

**Letter to DHS Secretary Kristi Noem (November 21, 2025):**
- Signed by 43 congressional representatives
- Led by Dave Min (D-Irvine) and Judy Chu (D-Pasadena)
- Accuses ICE of "gross negligence"
- Demands accountability for failure to meet basic detention standards

Key finding: **25 deaths by October 2025** vs **36 deaths in all of Trump's first term** (4 years). 2025 is deadliest year since 2004.

### Adelanto Facility Pattern

**Two deaths in two months at single facility:**
1. September 22, 2025: Ismael Ayala-Uribe (denied medical treatment)
2. October 23, 2025: Gabriel Garcia-Aviles (alcohol withdrawal neglect)

**Operator:** GEO Group (private contractor with history of medical neglect)

### ACLU Finding

> "95% of deaths in ICE detention could likely have been prevented with adequate medical care."

Garcia-Aviles' death fits this pattern - alcohol withdrawal is predictable and treatable.

### Investigations

- **Congressional:** 43 representatives demanding accountability
- **DHS OIG:** Not announced
- **Independent:** None
- **Accountability:** NONE (as of research date)

### Why This Matters

- Medical neglect in detention is systematic, not isolated
- Private contractor (GEO Group) incentivized by bed count, not care quality
- Family notification failures deny due process
- Physical trauma (head injuries) suggests abuse beyond neglect
- Congressional letters without enforcement don't prevent deaths

## Case 3: Kai Yin Wong - The Complex Medical Death

**Date:** October 25, 2025 (2 days after other two deaths)
**Location:** Methodist Metropolitan Hospital, San Antonio, Texas
**Age:** 63
**Nationality:** China (Lawful Permanent Resident since 1970)

### What Happened

Wong was in ICE custody for 18 months awaiting deportation after serving 14+ years in California state prison for continuous sexual abuse of a child. On October 11, 2025, he was emergency airlifted to hospital for heart failure and pneumonia. On October 12, he was transferred to Methodist Metropolitan Hospital for heart valve surgery. Surgery occurred October 23 but he developed cardiac tamponade (life-threatening complication). His family requested withdrawal of life support on October 25.

### Critical Facts

- **Convicted sex offender** - Complicates advocacy but doesn't negate ICE duty of care
- **18 months in ICE custody before crisis** - Questions about medical monitoring
- **High-risk surgery on unstable patient** - Valve replacement 12 days after emergency
- **Cardiac tamponade** - Serious surgical complication
- **Death within 2 days of surgery** - Suggests poor surgical outcome
- **Family present** - Unlike many cases, family made end-of-life decision
- **Asian detainee pattern** - 1 in 4 ICE deaths are Asian (disproportionate)

### Pattern Recognition

This is a **medical complications death**, where chronic conditions deteriorate in custody, leading to emergency intervention that fails. Key pattern:
1. Detainee with chronic condition in custody for extended period
2. Inadequate monitoring or care during detention
3. Acute medical crisis develops
4. Emergency hospitalization and intervention
5. Complications from delayed care or inappropriate treatment
6. Death in hospital, not detention facility
7. ICE attributes to medical condition, not custody failures

### The Criminal History Complication

Wong's conviction for child sex abuse must be acknowledged - these are serious crimes causing profound harm. However:

1. **He served his sentence** - 14+ years in California state prison
2. **Government custody obligations** - ICE has duty of care regardless of crime
3. **Medical ethics principle** - Care based on medical need, not moral judgment
4. **Pattern analysis** - His case reveals systemic issues affecting all detainees
5. **Eighth Amendment** - Even convicted criminals have right to adequate medical care

**ICE uses criminal history to deflect accountability:** Press release emphasized "convicted child sex offender" in headline, framing death as less concerning.

### Critical Medical Questions

1. Did Wong receive adequate cardiac care during 18 months in custody?
2. Was heart surgery medically appropriate given his acute condition?
3. Was informed consent properly obtained (he was intubated)?
4. Were family involved in surgical decision or only end-of-life decision?
5. Could medical management without surgery have prevented death?

### Investigations

- **None announced** - No DOJ, DHS OIG, Texas medical board inquiry
- **Why no scrutiny:**
  - Criminal history reduces public sympathy
  - Family's end-of-life decision provides cover
  - Death attributed to surgical complication, not neglect
  - Chinese national (less advocacy infrastructure)
- **Accountability:** NONE

### Why This Matters

- Criminal history shouldn't determine quality of medical care
- 18-month detention before crisis suggests monitoring failures
- Surgical complications after emergency intervention raise quality questions
- Asian detainees disproportionately dying in custody
- ICE uses "deservingness" to avoid accountability

## Cross-Cutting Patterns: October 2025

### Three Deaths, Three Patterns

1. **Pursuit Death (Castro Rivera):** ICE creates deadly situation without direct force
2. **Medical Neglect (Garcia-Aviles):** Preventable death from inadequate care
3. **Medical Complications (Wong):** Chronic condition deteriorates, intervention fails

### Common Threads

**All three cases involve:**
- ICE custody or enforcement actions
- Preventable deaths
- No accountability
- Systemic failures, not isolated incidents
- Deaths occurring in hospital, not at ICE facilities (allows ICE to distance itself)

### 2025: Deadliest Year Since 2004

**By October 2025:**
- **25 deaths** in ICE custody
- **36 deaths** in all of Trump's first term (4 years)
- 64% of first-term deaths in just 9 months
- 2025 deadliest year in two decades

**Contributing Factors:**
1. Increased detention population (mass deportation policies)
2. Decreased oversight (facility inspections down 36.25%)
3. Medical staffing issues (recruitment/training/turnover)
4. Systemic medical neglect (80+ documented cases)

### Accountability Landscape

**Federal Investigations:**
- Shooting deaths (Alex Pretti, Renée Good): Immediate DOJ investigations
- Pursuit deaths (Castro Rivera): No federal investigation
- Medical neglect deaths (Garcia-Aviles, Wong): No federal investigation

**Congressional Oversight:**
- 43 representatives signed letter on Garcia-Aviles case
- Accusations of "gross negligence"
- Demands for accountability
- **But:** Historical pattern shows congressional letters without enforcement don't prevent deaths

**Barriers to Accountability:**
1. Qualified immunity for ICE agents
2. Sovereign immunity for federal government
3. Private contractors (GEO Group) insulated by federal contracts
4. Self-investigation (DHS OIG investigates DHS)
5. Political climate prioritizing enforcement over safety
6. Media attention disparities (shootings > medical neglect)

### The "Deservingness" Problem

ICE and media coverage create hierarchy of victims:
- **U.S. citizens** (Alex Pretti): Maximum outrage, investigation
- **Long-term residents** (Garcia-Aviles): Some congressional attention
- **No removal order** (Castro Rivera): Advocacy but no federal action
- **Convicted criminals** (Wong): Minimal scrutiny, criminal history used to deflect

**Result:** Two-tier system where some detainees receive substandard care based on immigration status or criminal history, violating medical ethics and constitutional protections.

## Key Findings Across All Three Cases

### 1. ICE Enforcement Kills Multiple Ways

Not just shootings - pursuit situations, medical neglect, and inadequate care during medical crises all result in preventable deaths.

### 2. "Targeted" Operations Sweep Non-Targets

Castro Rivera had no removal order but was arrested during "targeted, intelligence-based operation." Raises questions about legal basis and scope of ICE authority.

### 3. Medical Neglect is Systematic

- 95% of ICE detention deaths preventable with adequate care (ACLU)
- Private contractors incentivized by detention, not care quality
- Inadequate medical screening, monitoring, and treatment
- Family notification failures deny due process

### 4. Accountability Gap

- Pursuit deaths: No investigation (contrast with shootings)
- Medical neglect: Congressional letters but no enforcement
- Systemic pattern: Deaths continue despite oversight

### 5. 2025 Death Surge

25 deaths by October vs 36 in entire first Trump term. This is not normal variance - it's a crisis.

### 6. Private Contractors & Profit Motive

GEO Group operates Adelanto (Garcia-Aviles death, pattern of neglect). Private prison model creates perverse incentives: more detainees = more profit, regardless of medical capacity or outcomes.

### 7. Language & Cultural Barriers

Wong (Chinese) and Garcia-Aviles (Spanish-speaking) likely faced communication barriers affecting medical care quality and informed consent.

### 8. Detention Duration & Medical Risk

Wong in custody 18 months before crisis. Garcia-Aviles arrested with active medical condition. Prolonged detention without adequate medical monitoring increases death risk.

## Conclusion

October 2025 saw at least three preventable deaths due to ICE enforcement and detention. Each represents a different way immigration enforcement kills:

**Josué Castro Rivera** died fleeing agents who had no removal order for him, struck by a vehicle on a highway ICE should have known was dangerous.

**Gabriel Garcia-Aviles** died from alcohol withdrawal syndrome - a treatable condition - after ICE arrested him in acute medical crisis, transferred him to a facility with a pattern of medical neglect, and didn't notify his family for 8 days while he lay dying.

**Kai Yin Wong** died from complications of heart valve surgery performed while in ICE custody, raising questions about whether 18 months of detention included adequate cardiac monitoring and whether surgery was appropriate for an unstable 63-year-old patient.

None of these deaths have resulted in accountability. None are being federally investigated. The families are left to grieve, advocate, and wonder what happened.

This is the machinery of immigration enforcement in 2025: arrests without individualized probable cause, medical neglect in profit-driven private facilities, pursuit situations that foreseeably end in death, and a complete absence of consequences for the agencies and agents responsible.

**These deaths were preventable.**
**These deaths are being repeated.**
**Documentation is resistance.**
