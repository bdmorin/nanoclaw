## Executive Summary

On January 24, 2026, Alex Jeffrey Pretti, a 37-year-old ICU nurse and U.S. citizen, was shot and killed by U.S. Customs and Border Protection agents in Minneapolis, Minnesota. The incident occurred during immigration enforcement operations and has led to multiple investigations, including a DOJ civil rights probe. No criminal charges have been filed against Pretti - he is the victim in this case.

## 2. THE INCIDENT - January 24, 2026

**Location:** Near East 26th Street and Nicollet Avenue, South Minneapolis

**Timeline:**

- **~9:00 AM CT:** U.S. Customs and Border Protection conducting targeted immigration operation
- **9:00:44 AM:** Pretti holds phone in front of federal officer, filming
- **9:00:50 AM:** Officer pepper-sprays Pretti; Pretti raises hand toward officer
- **9:00:54 AM:** Pretti falls into person with backpack, possibly to stabilize himself
- **Shortly after:** Multiple agents pin Pretti to the ground; one agent seen hitting him
- **During struggle:** Border Patrol Agent yells "He's got a gun!" multiple times
- **~9:03 AM:** 10 shots fired in less than 5 seconds
- **9:03 AM:** Minneapolis Police receive shooting reports
- **9:05 AM:** Emergency personnel arrive
- **Shortly after:** Pretti declared dead at scene

**What Pretti Was Doing:**
- Filming law enforcement with his phone
- Directing traffic
- Intervened when agent pushed a woman to the ground - stood between them with arm around the woman

## 4. INVESTIGATIONS

### Federal Investigation

**Department of Justice Civil Rights Division:**
- Opened investigation on January 30, 2026
- Led by FBI with DOJ Civil Rights Division lawyers
- Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) in secondary role
- Investigating potential violations of federal civil rights statutes
- Focus: Whether agents violated Fourth Amendment (unreasonable seizures, excessive force)

### State Investigation - Major Conflicts

**Minnesota Bureau of Criminal Apprehension (BCA):**
- Attempted to investigate (standard for all MN law enforcement shootings)
- **Denied access to scene TWICE by federal agents**
- Had warrant but was turned away
- State hasn't ruled out bringing charges against federal officers

**Legal Disputes:**
- Minnesota and Hennepin County sued in federal court
- Sought to compel evidence preservation
- Federal judge declined to compel federal officials to preserve all evidence
- Judge later lifted restraining order allowing feds to collect evidence

### Agents Identified (ProPublica)

A **ProPublica** investigation identified the two federal agents who fired shots:
- **Jesus Ochoa** — U.S. Border Patrol Agent
- **Raymundo Gutierrez** — U.S. Customs and Border Protection Officer

DHS declined to confirm the names. Both remain on duty/administrative status as of the DOJ investigation.

*Source: ProPublica reporting, February 2026*

### Internal Review Contradictions

A preliminary government internal review contradicts the Trump administration's initial narrative:
- **No mention** of Pretti attacking officers
- **No mention** of Pretti threatening agents with a weapon
- Conflicts with administration's initial description
