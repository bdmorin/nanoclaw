## Executive Summary

On October 21, 2025, Carlitos Ricardo Parias, a 44-year-old Mexican national known as "Richard LA" on TikTok with 340,000+ followers documenting ICE operations, was shot in the elbow by an ICE agent during an immigration enforcement stop in South Los Angeles. A deputy U.S. Marshal was also wounded by a ricochet bullet.

Parias was indicted for assault on a federal officer, but **charges were dismissed WITH PREJUDICE** on December 28, 2025 by Judge Fernando Olguin due to:
1. Denial of counsel - authorities repeatedly blocked his lawyers from visiting him in detention
2. Government failure to release body camera footage

**Critical finding:** Body camera footage appears to contradict government claims - video shows agent smashing Parias' window while his car was stationary, contradicting claims he was "ramming" vehicles. Despite the criminal case dismissal, Parias remains in ICE detention.

## 2. INCIDENT TIMELINE

### October 21, 2025 - The Shooting

**Time:** 8:52 AM
**Location:** 400 block of East 20th Street near Trinity Street, South Los Angeles

**Government's Version:**
- Targeted enforcement traffic stop on undocumented immigrant
- Parias drove vehicle back and forth, striking law enforcement vehicles
- Attempting to evade immigration arrest
- Agent fired in response to vehicle threat

**What Body Camera Shows:**
- Agent approached Parias' passenger side door
- Agent almost immediately stated he was going to "break the window"
- Agent smashed window
- Car began to rev, smoke billowed
- **Parias' car did NOT appear to be moving** at time agents claimed ramming
- Other agents positioned beside driver's side door

**The Shooting:**
- ICE agent fired weapon
- Parias shot in elbow
- Ricochet bullet struck deputy U.S. Marshal in hand/head (reports vary)

**Key Discrepancy:**
Government claimed Parias rammed vehicles; body camera shows car stationary when agent smashed window and fired.
