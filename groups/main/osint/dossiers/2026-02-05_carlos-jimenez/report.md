## Executive Summary

On October 30, 2025, Carlos Jimenez, a 24-year-old U.S. citizen, food bank worker, and father of three, was shot by an ICE agent in Ontario, California near a school bus stop on Vineyard Avenue.

**Critical finding:** According to his attorneys, Jimenez had stopped to warn ICE agents that children would soon be gathering at a nearby bus stop. When he tried to leave (his path blocked by ICE vehicles), he was shot. He was shot **from behind** while driving away.

Jimenez still has **bullet fragments lodged in his shoulder and torso**. After being shot, the FBI interrogated him **while he was bleeding and before receiving substantial medical treatment**. He was indicted for assault on a federal officer and faces up to 20 years in prison. He pleaded not guilty.

Congresswoman Norma Torres has demanded accountability.

## 2. INCIDENT TIMELINE

### October 30, 2025 - The Shooting

**Time:** ~6:30 AM
**Location:** 4900 block of Vineyard Avenue (near Baseline Avenue), Ontario, California
- Near elementary school bus stop

**Context:**
- Jimenez driving to work at food pantry
- ICE conducting unrelated immigration enforcement traffic stop nearby
- School bus stop where children would soon gather

**Jimenez's Account (via attorneys):**

1. Jimenez noticed ICE vehicles blocking area near school bus stop
2. Pulled alongside to warn agents children would soon arrive
3. Spoke through passenger window about the kids
4. **"The male officer immediately had his gun drawn and pointed at Mr. Jimenez's head and told him to get the f' out of here"**
5. Jimenez tried to leave
6. His path was blocked by ICE vehicles
7. Had to drive forward, then back at angle, then forward to navigate around
8. Agent shot him **as he was driving away**

**DHS/ICE Account:**

1. Jimenez engaged in verbal altercation with officers
2. Pulled forward, turning wheels
3. Rapidly accelerated backward
4. Agent "feared" Jimenez would hit him and another car
5. Agent fired "defensive shots"

**The Shooting:**
- Agent fired at Jimenez
- Shot in shoulder
- Bullet fragments lodged in shoulder AND torso
- Shot from behind per attorneys
