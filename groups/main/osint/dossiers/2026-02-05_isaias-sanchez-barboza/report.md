## Executive Summary

On December 11, 2025, Isaias Sanchez Barboza, a 31-year-old Mexican national, was shot and killed by a U.S. Border Patrol agent near Rio Grande City, Texas. The agent reported being in a physical struggle with Barboza for approximately two minutes before firing three shots. Barboza was pronounced dead at Starr County Hospital approximately 50 minutes after the shooting. The Texas Rangers are investigating.

## FINAL ASSESSMENT

**Overall Confidence: HIGH**

Multiple sources confirm key facts. CBP released detailed incident report with timeline. Name confirmed as Isaias Sanchez Barboza.

**Assessment:**
This case differs from vehicle-related shootings. It involves a physical altercation where the agent claims self-defense during a struggle. Without body camera footage or independent witnesses, the agent's account is the only version of events during the critical two minutes.

**Key Concerns:**
- No body camera footage referenced
- CBP pre-emptively framed narrative before investigation
- Three shots during alleged struggle raises proportionality questions
- Dehumanizing language in official release
