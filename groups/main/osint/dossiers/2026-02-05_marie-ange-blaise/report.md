## Executive Summary

On April 25, 2025, Marie Ange Blaise, a 44-year-old Haitian woman, died at the Broward Transitional Center in Pompano Beach, Florida, while in ICE custody. She had been detained for 72 days after being stopped at an airport in the U.S. Virgin Islands. Medical examiner ruled death was caused by atherosclerotic and hypertensive cardiovascular disease. Family and witnesses allege medical neglect, claiming she complained of chest pain for weeks but was denied medical care and faced delayed emergency response. ICE claims she repeatedly refused medication. The case sparked congressional inquiry and highlights systemic issues in for-profit detention medical care.

## 3. MEDICAL TIMELINE AT BROWARD TRANSITIONAL CENTER

**April 5, 2025 - Arrival:**
- Initial medical screening conducted
- Vital signs assessed
- Blood pressure medication continued

**Early-Mid April:**
- Blood pressure monitoring showed elevated readings on multiple dates
- April 15 notes: "Non-compliant with blood pressure medication"
- ICE claims: "Refused blood pressure medication nine times" at Louisiana facility

**Weeks Before Death:**
- **Son's account:** Mother complained of chest pain for "about a month"
- **Son's account:** Mother complained of abdominal cramps
- **Family claim:** Requested to see physician, was refused

**April 25, 2025 - Day of Death:**

**Hours Before Death:**
- Blaise reported chest pain and abdominal cramps to detention staff
- **Son's account (2:54 PM California time call):** "She asked detention staff to see a physician, they refused her"
- **Congressional report (Rep. Cherfilus-McCormick):** "Marie had been complaining about chest pain for hours. They gave her some pills and told her to go lie down."

**6:00 PM:**
- Took one of two prescribed blood pressure medications

**8:00 PM:**
- Took blood pressure medication again

**~9:00 PM - Collapse:**
- Blaise in room with three other detainees
- Witnesses say she "looked different" and "seemed off"
- Suddenly became unresponsive, collapsed

**Emergency Response:**
- **Witness account (via Human Rights Watch):** "We started yelling for help, but the guards ignored us"
- **Witness:** One officer "approached slowly, looked at her without intervening, and then walked away"
- **8 minutes elapsed:** Before medical provider arrived
- **Additional 15-20 minutes:** Before rescue team arrived
- **Total delay:** 23-28 minutes from collapse to rescue team arrival
- Staff attempted resuscitation using automated external defibrillator (AED)

**8:35 PM:**
- Pronounced dead by medical professionals at Broward Transitional Center

## 6. PROTOCOL VIOLATIONS & CONCERNS

### ICE Medical Standards (PBNDS 2011/2019)

**Required Standards:**
1. **Timely access to medical care** for reported symptoms
2. **Immediate emergency response** for life-threatening conditions
3. **Proper monitoring** of chronic conditions like hypertension
4. **Documentation** of all medical requests and care provided

**Potential Violations:**

**1. Denial of Requested Medical Care:**
- If son's account is accurate, refusal to allow physician visit for chest pain complaints violates standard
- "Go lie down" response to cardiac symptoms inadequate

**2. Emergency Response Delay:**
- 8-minute delay for on-site medical provider unacceptable
- 23-28 minute total delay for cardiac event potentially fatal
- Guard walking away from unresponsive detainee potential dereliction

**3. Medication Administration Disputes:**
- ICE claims 9 refusals; family claims denial of care
- Records show she took medication day of death
- Conflicting accounts raise documentation reliability questions

**4. Hospitalization Documentation Gap:**
- Son claims Louisiana hospitalization for hypertension
- ICE records make no mention
- If true, represents serious record-keeping failure
