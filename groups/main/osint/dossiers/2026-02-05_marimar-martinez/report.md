## Executive Summary

On October 4, 2025, Marimar Martinez, a 30-year-old U.S. citizen and teaching assistant, was shot five times by Border Patrol Agent Charles Exum during an immigration enforcement action in Brighton Park, Chicago. The incident occurred during "Operation Midway Blitz." Martinez survived with seven wounds. Federal prosecutors initially charged her with assaulting federal officers, but **charges were dismissed with prejudice** on November 20, 2025 after:

1. Agent Exum's text messages revealed he bragged "I fired 5 rounds and she had 7 holes"
2. Body camera footage contradicted the government's version of events
3. Evidence tampering concerns: Exum drove the evidence vehicle 1,000+ miles to Maine where a mechanic attempted to remove scuff marks

Martinez has since testified before Congress and become an advocate for DHS reform.

## 2. INCIDENT TIMELINE

### October 4, 2025 - The Shooting

**Location:** Near 39th Street and Kedzie Avenue, Brighton Park, Chicago

**Government's Initial Version:**
- Martinez and co-defendant Anthony Ian Santos Ruiz (21) were part of civilian convoy following Border Patrol
- Martinez allegedly rammed Agent Exum's Chevrolet Tahoe
- Vehicles boxed in agents' vehicle
- Martinez continued driving toward agents
- Agent Exum exited vehicle and fired in self-defense

**Defense Version (supported by bodycam):**
- Federal agents rammed Martinez's car
- After collision, Martinez stopped her vehicle
- She accelerated away, swerving LEFT to avoid agents exiting their vehicle
- Agents opened fire as she drove AWAY, not toward them

**The Shooting:**
- Agent Charles Exum fired 5 rounds
- Martinez struck 7 times (arm, chest, both legs)
- She survived

**Immediate Aftermath:**
- Martinez discharged from hospital after less than 3 hours
- Taken into FBI custody immediately after
- Agent took cell phone photo of her at hospital without consent, over her objections

## 4. EVIDENCE ISSUES

### Vehicle Evidence Tampering

**The Problem:**
- After the shooting, Agent Exum was allowed to drive his Chevrolet Tahoe (the "evidence vehicle") more than 1,000 miles from Chicago to his home base in Maine

**Government's Shifting Story:**
- Initially told court: Exum took SUV to Maine because it was his "personal vehicle"
- Later filing: The car is actually part of official Border Patrol fleet
- Judge noted she had not been told a mechanic had worked on the car

**Maine Mechanic:**
- Border Patrol mechanic in Maine attempted to "wipe off" scuff marks from the crash
- Government claimed no actual repairs or alterations were made
- Defense argued this potentially destroyed evidence

### Body Camera Footage

- Footage reportedly contradicts Border Patrol's version of events
- Attorney for Martinez sought public release
- Prosecutors eventually dropped opposition to release
- Footage shows agent's statement "Do something, b----" before shooting
