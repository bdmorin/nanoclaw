## Executive Summary

Parady La, a 46-year-old Cambodian American father and lifelong U.S. resident, died on January 9, 2026, three days after ICE agents surrounded his car while he was driving to the grocery store. He had been a lawful permanent resident since 1982 — arriving as a two-year-old refugee from a Thai camp after his family fled the Khmer Rouge genocide.

Detained at FDC Philadelphia, La experienced severe fentanyl withdrawal. His family says he **begged for help for 24 hours**, vomiting repeatedly without receiving water or appropriate medical treatment. On January 7, he was found unresponsive in his cell. Staff administered CPR and multiple doses of NARCAN — a treatment for opioid *overdose*, not withdrawal, raising questions about whether staff understood his condition at all.

Hospitalized at Thomas Jefferson University Hospital, La was diagnosed with anoxic brain injury, cardiac arrest, shock, and multiple organ failures. He was pronounced dead January 9 after complete renal failure and loss of all brain activity.

**Key finding:** A man with a known substance dependence condition was detained by ICE and left to undergo life-threatening withdrawal without adequate medical supervision for at least 24 hours. The administration of NARCAN to someone in withdrawal (not overdose) suggests fundamental incompetence in medical care. This is the fourth death at a Pennsylvania detention facility since December 2023.

## 2. INCIDENT TIMELINE

### January 6, 2026 — Arrest
- La left his home in Upper Darby to go to Fresh Grocer grocery store
- When he didn't return after several hours, family grew concerned
- Family tracked his car — found it abandoned approximately half a mile from his house
- La later called family: ICE agents had surrounded his car, asked for ID, and detained him
- Transferred to Federal Detention Center (FDC) Philadelphia

### January 6-7 — Untreated Withdrawal
- La experienced severe drug withdrawal symptoms in custody
- **Family account:** He begged for help for approximately 24 hours
- Continuous vomiting without being given water or appropriate medical treatment
- ICE characterization: He was "receiving treatment for severe drug withdrawal"
- **Critical discrepancy:** Family and ICE accounts directly contradict on adequacy of care

### January 7, 2026 — Found Unresponsive
- FDC officers discovered La unresponsive in his cell
- Staff administered CPR
- **Multiple doses of NARCAN administered**
  - NARCAN (naloxone) reverses opioid *overdose* — it does NOT treat withdrawal
  - If La was in withdrawal (not overdose), NARCAN was medically inappropriate
  - This suggests staff either misdiagnosed his condition or were following incorrect protocols
- Emergency medical services called
- Admitted to Thomas Jefferson University Hospital that evening in critical condition

### January 8, 2026 — Hospital
- Diagnosed with:
  - **Anoxic brain injury** (brain damage from oxygen deprivation)
  - Post cardiac arrest
  - Shock
  - Multiple organ failures
- Admitted to Neuro Intensive Care Unit
- Staff determined: complete renal failure, no remaining brain activity

### January 9, 2026 — Death
- Parady La pronounced dead at Thomas Jefferson University Hospital
- Cause: organ failure and brain death following cardiac arrest in custody

## 3. MEDICAL NEGLECT ANALYSIS

### The NARCAN Question
- NARCAN (naloxone) is used to reverse opioid *overdose* — blocking opioid receptors
- In withdrawal, the problem is the *absence* of opioids, not their presence
- Administering NARCAN to someone in withdrawal would have **no therapeutic effect** and could potentially worsen symptoms
- Proper withdrawal management requires: IV fluids, anti-emetics, clonidine or buprenorphine, vital sign monitoring, and potentially ICU-level care
- The use of NARCAN suggests staff did not understand or properly assess La's condition

### 24-Hour Neglect Pattern
- Opioid withdrawal can be life-threatening, especially fentanyl withdrawal
- Severe dehydration from prolonged vomiting without fluid replacement → electrolyte imbalance → cardiac arrest
- This is a known, predictable, treatable medical emergency
- 24 hours of vomiting without IV fluids is a textbook failure of duty of care

### Systemic Context
- ICE halted payments to medical contractors October 3, 2025
- Payments unlikely to resume until end of April 2026
- FDC Philadelphia is a federal facility (BOP), not a dedicated ICE detention center
- Federal prisons are not equipped for immigration detainees with acute medical needs

## 5. PATTERN ANALYSIS

### Cambodian/Southeast Asian Targeting
- ICE has specifically targeted Cambodian, Vietnamese, Hmong, and Lao communities
- Many are refugees or children of refugees from US-caused conflicts (Vietnam War, Khmer Rouge)
- Focus on decades-old criminal convictions
- Community has deep roots in US — many arrived as babies/children

### Medical Neglect Deaths
- Parady La joins a pattern of ICE custody deaths from inadequate medical care:
  - Nhon Ngoc Nguyen (55, Vietnam, April 2025) — pneumonia during detention
  - Francisco Gaspar-Andres (48, Guatemala, Dec 2025) — 54-day delay in care
  - Pete Sumalo Montejo (72, Philippines, Dec 2025) — septic shock after 284 days
  - Nenko Stanev Gantchev (56, Bulgaria, Dec 2025) — untreated diabetes

### Withdrawal Deaths in Custody
- Known, preventable cause of death in detention settings
- Fentanyl withdrawal is more dangerous than most opioid withdrawal
- Proper protocols exist but require trained medical staff and equipment
- Federal detention facilities routinely fail to meet standard of care
