## Executive Summary

On October 17, 2025, Phillip M. Brown, a 33-year-old Black man from Hyattsville, Maryland, was shot at by a Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) agent during a traffic stop in Washington, D.C. The agent fired at least three rounds into Brown's vehicle - two bullets struck the passenger seat, one pierced Brown's jacket collar. Brown was not physically injured.

**Key finding:** A documented cover-up occurred. MPD Officer Jason Sterling testified in court that he was **instructed by a "team leader" to omit the shooting from his police report**. The U.S. Attorney prosecuting Brown reportedly never received body camera footage and was unaware the shooting even occurred when offering a plea deal.

Brown was jailed for 4 days on a fleeing charge, which was **dismissed** on October 21, 2025. Federal prosecutors **declined to charge** the HSI agent because Brown wasn't struck by gunfire.

## 3. THE COVER-UP

### Police Officer Testimony

**Officer Jason Sterling (MPD):**
- Testified at preliminary hearing
- Stated he was **instructed by a "team leader"** not to include details about the federal agent's shooting in his report
- Reason given: "upcoming internal affairs investigation"
- **Omitted the shooting from his court affidavit**

**Officer Divonnie Powell (MPD):**
- Sterling's partner
- **Excluded the shooting from the public incident report**

### Prosecutor Kept in Dark

**U.S. Attorney Handling Case:**
- Reportedly never received body camera footage
- Was **unaware of the shooting** when offering Brown a plea deal
- Defense only learned of shooting at preliminary hearing

### Defense Response

**Attorney Quo Mieko S. Judkins:**
> "I've never heard of that"
(Regarding instruction to omit critical details)

Noted prosecutors must disclose such information to defense under Brady obligations.

**Attorneys Bernadette Armand and E. Paige White:**
- Provided photos of bullet holes to media
- Called omission evidence of cover-up attempt

## FINAL ASSESSMENT

**Overall Confidence: HIGH**

**What We Know For Certain:**
- Phillip Brown, 33, Black man from Maryland, shot at by HSI agent Oct 17, 2025
- At least 3 rounds fired into his vehicle during traffic stop
- Bullet pierced his jacket collar - narrowly missed him
- MPD officer testified he was INSTRUCTED to omit shooting from report
- U.S. Attorney prosecuting Brown was unaware shooting occurred
- Charges against Brown DISMISSED Oct 21, 2025
- Charges against HSI agent DECLINED - because Brown wasn't hit

**Significance:**
This case documents a **verified cover-up**:
1. Officer testified under oath he was told to omit shooting
2. Police reports excluded the shooting
3. Prosecutor wasn't informed
4. Only revealed when defense learned of it at hearing

The "not hit" justification for declining charges is particularly troubling - it creates a system where shooting AT people has no consequences as long as you miss. Combined with the documented cover-up instructions, this case reveals systemic accountability failures.
