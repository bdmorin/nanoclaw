## Executive Summary

On May 18, 2023, Raymond Mattia, a 58-year-old member of the Tohono O'odham Nation, was shot and killed by U.S. Border Patrol agents outside his home in Menager's Dam, Arizona. Agents fired approximately **38 shots**, hitting Mattia 9 times.

**Critical finding:** Mattia was **UNARMED** at the time of shooting. He had thrown a sheathed machete/knife to the ground and raised his hands when agents opened fire. Body camera footage shows agents issuing conflicting commands before unleashing the fusillade.

The Department of Justice **declined to prosecute** any agents. The Tohono O'odham Nation called the decision "a travesty of justice." The family filed a $15 million wrongful death lawsuit naming three agents: Scott Whitehouse, Dan Sifuentes, and Ivan Torralva.

## 2. INCIDENT TIMELINE

### May 18, 2023 - The Night of the Shooting

**Location:** Menager's Dam Village, Tohono O'odham Nation (southwest of Tucson, near U.S.-Mexico border)

**Initial Call:**
- 911 call reporting dispute and possible gunshots at Mattia's residence
- Tohono O'odham Nation Police Department responded
- Tribal police requested Border Patrol assistance

**Alternative Account (Family):**
- Mattia had called Border Patrol because migrants trespassed on his property
- Wanted assistance removing them

**The Confrontation:**

1. Border Patrol agents arrived, moved through dark property
2. Agents confronted Mattia
3. **Conflicting commands** issued by officers
4. Mattia threw sheathed machete/knife - landed near tribal police officer
5. Mattia raised his hands/extended arm
6. Agents opened fire

**The Shooting:**
- Agents fired approximately **38 rounds**
- Mattia struck **9 times**
- Mattia was **UNARMED** when shot - no firearm found
- He had already thrown the knife away
- Died shortly after

**Sister's Account:**
- Annette Mattia heard gunshots from her adjacent home
- Describes ongoing trauma and anxiety around Border Patrol presence

## 5. LEGAL PROCEEDINGS

### Criminal Prosecution

**DOJ Decision:** Declined to prosecute
- Determined shooting did not violate state or federal criminal law
- No agents charged

**Tribal Response:**
- Tohono O'odham Nation called decision "a travesty of justice"
- Protested the decision
- Demanded accountability

### Civil Lawsuit

**Filed:** May 2024 (nearly one year after incident)
**Claim:** $15 million wrongful death tort claim
**Defendants:**
- U.S. Government
- Three named Border Patrol agents

**Claims (8 total):**
- Excessive use of force
- Assault
- Battery
- Negligence
- Constitutional rights violations (Bivens)
- Federal Tort Claims Act violations

**Status (as of 2025):**
- Filed in federal court, Tucson
- DOJ seeking dismissal of various aspects
- Judge allowed case to partially move forward (August 2025)
- No resolution reached
