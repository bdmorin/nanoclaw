## Executive Summary

On September 12, 2025, Silverio Villegas González, a 38-year-old Mexican immigrant and father of two, was fatally shot by an ICE agent during a traffic stop in Franklin Park, Illinois (Chicago suburb). The case became highly controversial when bodycam footage revealed the ICE agent described his injuries as "nothing major" - directly contradicting DHS claims that the agent was "seriously injured" and "dragged a significant distance." The Cook County Medical Examiner ruled the death a **homicide**. The bullet entered the back-left of his neck on a downward trajectory, suggesting he was shot from above while in his vehicle. Congressional members demanded OIG investigations and Rep. Delia Ramirez moved to subpoena DHS Secretary Kristi Noem.

## 2. INCIDENT TIMELINE

**September 12, 2025:**

**Morning:**
- Villegas González dropped off his child at school

**~Time Unknown - Traffic Stop:**
- ICE agents initiated traffic stop near Grand Avenue and Elder Lane, Franklin Park, IL
- Part of "Operation Midway Blitz" enforcement operation

**The Incident:**
- DHS claims Villegas González attempted to flee and struck an ICE agent with his vehicle
- Witness (truck driver) says he saw car reverse after being cut off by agents
- One ICE agent fired weapon
- Villegas González's vehicle crashed into truck trailer
- One gunshot heard by witness

**Shooting:**
- Single fatal shot struck Villegas González
- Bullet entered left side of neck, traveled downward, lodged in lower-right chest
- Graze wounds on left pinky and ring fingers
- Shot from above, driver's side (suggesting shooter above victim in vehicle)

**Death:**
- Pronounced dead at hospital
- Drug paraphernalia found on person at hospital

## 3. EVIDENCE CONTRADICTIONS

### DHS Official Statement (September 12, 2025)
- Agent was "hit by the car and dragged a significant distance"
- Agent "sustained multiple injuries"
- Agent was "in stable condition"
- Agent "feared for his life"
- Shooting was "self-defense"

### Bodycam Footage (Released September 22-23, 2025)
- ICE agent on scene describes injuries as **"nothing major"**
- Reported injuries: "left knee injury and some lacerations to his hands"
- Agent was released from hospital same day

### Witness Account (Truck Driver)
- Did NOT see vehicle dragging any ICE agents
- Saw Villegas González reverse after being cut off by agents
- Heard one gunshot
- Stated: "I don't know if they shot him beforehand...but I'm assuming he had gotten shot because why else would he crash into the trailer?"

### Critical Gap
- **ICE agents were NOT wearing body cameras during the actual shooting**
- Only Franklin Park Police bodycam captured aftermath

## FINAL ASSESSMENT

**Overall Confidence: HIGH**

**What We Know For Certain:**
- Silverio Villegas González, 38, father of two, was shot and killed by ICE on September 12, 2025
- He had NO criminal record - only minor traffic violations
- Medical examiner ruled death a HOMICIDE
- Bullet trajectory (downward, through neck) suggests shot from above while in vehicle
- ICE agent described his own injuries as "nothing major" on bodycam
- DHS had claimed agent was "seriously injured" and "dragged a significant distance"
- ICE agents were NOT wearing body cameras during the shooting
- Congress demanded OIG investigation and moved to subpoena DHS Secretary

**Significance:**
This case exemplifies the pattern of DHS claims being contradicted by video evidence. The discrepancy between the agent's own casual description of minor injuries and DHS's official "seriously injured/dragged" narrative is documented on police bodycam footage. Combined with the homicide ruling and bullet trajectory suggesting the victim was shot while seated in his vehicle, this case raises serious questions about the use of lethal force and the accuracy of official accounts.
