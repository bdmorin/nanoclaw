## Executive Summary

Victor Manuel Diaz, a 36-year-old Nicaraguan national, died on January 14, 2026, at Camp East Montana — the nation's largest immigration detention facility, built on Fort Bliss Army base in El Paso, Texas. He had been detained for only 8 days, apprehended in Minneapolis on January 6 while working at a Korean restaurant during Operation Metro Surge.

ICE claims he died of a "presumed suicide." His family in Nicaragua says they do not believe he took his own life.

**The critical finding is not how Diaz died, but what happened after:** When the El Paso County Medical Examiner ruled the *previous* Camp East Montana death (Geraldo Lunas Campos, January 3) a **homicide**, ICE redirected Diaz's body to **William Beaumont Army Medical Center** — a military hospital that does not release autopsy reports to the public. The El Paso County Medical Examiner's office told Diaz's attorney they attempted to take custody of the body but were blocked by the federal government in a "jurisdictional dispute." The family was never notified of the transfer.

This represents deliberate circumvention of independent civilian oversight after that oversight produced an inconvenient truth.

Diaz was the **third person to die at Camp East Montana in six weeks**. The facility is operated by a contractor with no prior corrections experience, under a $1.2 billion contract, on the site of a former Japanese internment camp.

## 2. INCIDENT TIMELINE

### January 6, 2026 — Arrest (Minneapolis)
- Diaz apprehended by ICE in Minneapolis while working at Korean restaurant
- Part of **Operation Metro Surge** — described by DHS as "the largest immigration enforcement operation ever"
- Operation launched December 2025, initially targeting Twin Cities

### January 6-14, 2026 — Transfer and Detention
- Transferred from Minneapolis to Camp East Montana, El Paso, Texas
- **Transport distance:** ~1,200 miles
- Detained at Camp East Montana for approximately 8 days before death
- No records released of mental health screening or medical evaluations during this period

### January 14, 2026 — Death
- ICE reports Diaz was "found unconscious and unresponsive in his room"
- Cause: **"Presumed suicide"** (ICE characterization)
- No details released about method, time, or circumstances
- Third death at Camp East Montana in six weeks

### January 14+ — Autopsy Manipulation
- Diaz's body **NOT sent to El Paso County Medical Examiner** (who had jurisdiction and had performed the two prior Camp East Montana autopsies)
- Instead sent to **William Beaumont Army Medical Center** at Fort Bliss
- El Paso County ME's office told attorney Randall Kallinen they **attempted to take custody** of the body
- Federal government blocked transfer in a "jurisdictional dispute"
- **Diaz's family was never notified** of the body being sent to the military hospital
- William Beaumont **does not release autopsy reports to the public** — only to family and investigators

### January 15+ — Visitor Denial
- Starting the day after Diaz's death, visitors to Camp East Montana were told they **could not see Minnesota detainees**
- Guards told visitors: "They're not allowing the people from Minnesota to have visitors"
- Sources requested anonymity fearing loss of visitation privileges
- Denial continued through the weekend

## 3. AUTOPSY MANIPULATION

### The Pattern

**Death 1: Francisco Gaspar-Andres (Dec 3, 2025)**
- Autopsy performed by El Paso County Medical Examiner
- Cause: suspected liver/kidney failure
- No controversy over routing

**Death 2: Geraldo Lunas Campos (Jan 3, 2026)**
- Autopsy performed by El Paso County Medical Examiner
- **Dr. Adam Gonzalez** found: asphyxia due to neck and torso compression
- **Ruled: HOMICIDE**
- ICE's narrative destroyed: they had claimed suicide, then medical distress

**Death 3: Victor Manuel Diaz (Jan 14, 2026)**
- Body sent to **William Beaumont Army Medical Center** (military)
- El Paso County ME blocked from taking custody
- Military hospital does not release autopsy reports publicly
- Family not notified of the redirect

### Analysis

The sequence is clear:
1. Independent civilian ME performs autopsy → rules homicide → ICE narrative collapses
2. Next death at same facility: body routed to military hospital with no public reporting requirement
3. This circumvents the independent oversight mechanism that produced the homicide ruling

**Angélica César, Human Rights Watch fellow:** "The concern isn't just about where Diaz's autopsy is being done, but really who controls the process and whether it's independent from [DHS's] custodial authority."

## 4. CAMP EAST MONTANA — FACILITY PROFILE

### Overview
- **Location:** Fort Bliss Army base, El Paso, Texas
- **Type:** Tent-based detention facility (largest in the nation)
- **Capacity:** 2,700+ detainees
- **Opened:** August 2025 (despite congressional and advocacy warnings)
- **Contract:** $1.2 billion
- **Operator:** **Acquisition Logistics LLC** — private contractor headquartered in a **single-family home in Richmond, Virginia**
  - **No prior experience running a corrections facility** (AP investigation)
  - Subcontracted operations to other companies

### Historical Context
- Fort Bliss was a **Japanese internment site during WWII** (ACLU)
- Now houses the nation's largest immigration detention center
- Historical resonance: concentration camps on former concentration camp grounds

### Three Deaths in Six Weeks
| # | Name | Date | Age | Country | Cause | ME |

| 3 | Victor Manuel Diaz | Jan 14, 2026 | 36 | Nicaragua | "Presumed suicide" | Military hospital |
