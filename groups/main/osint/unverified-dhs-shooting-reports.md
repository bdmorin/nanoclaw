# DHS Officer-Involved Shootings: January 2025–January 2026

Federal immigration enforcement under DHS has resulted in at least **6 confirmed fatal shootings** and **8 non-fatal shootings** of civilians by ICE, CBP, and Border Patrol agents between January 2025 and January 2026. Several incidents have sparked controversy, with video evidence contradicting official accounts and federal charges being dismissed against shooting victims. This represents a significant escalation, with The Trace identifying 27 gun-related incidents involving immigration agents during this period—13 of which were shootings.

---

## Fatal shootings show contested circumstances across agencies

**1. Felix Bauckholt**
- **Date:** January 20, 2025
- **Location:** Coventry, Vermont (Interstate 91)
- **Agency:** U.S. Border Patrol (Swanton Sector)
- **Outcome:** Fatal
- **Circumstances:** During a traffic stop, passenger Teresa Youngblut allegedly opened fire first, killing Border Patrol Agent David Maland. Agents returned fire, killing Bauckholt, a German national. Both Bauckholt and Youngblut were members of a group called "Zizian." Youngblut has been indicted for murder with prosecutors seeking the death penalty.

**2. Raymond Mattia**
- **Date:** May 18, 2025
- **Location:** Meneger's Dam Village, Tohono O'odham Nation, Arizona
- **Agency:** U.S. Border Patrol (Tucson Sector)
- **Outcome:** Fatal
- **Circumstances:** Three Border Patrol agents responded to a shots-fired call. Mattia, **58**, a U.S. citizen and Tohono O'odham tribal member, was outside his residence when agents encountered him. He threw an object and "abruptly extended his right arm." Agents believed he had a firearm and opened fire. **No weapon was found on Mattia.** The U.S. Attorney's Office declined to prosecute the agents. The Tohono O'odham Nation called the decision "a travesty of justice."

**3. Ryan Louis Mosqueda** *(Attacker killed by agents)*
- **Date:** July 7, 2025
- **Location:** McAllen, Texas (Border Patrol facility)
- **Agency:** U.S. Border Patrol (Rio Grande Valley Sector)
- **Outcome:** Fatal
- **Circumstances:** Mosqueda, **27**, a U.S. citizen from Michigan, attacked the Border Patrol facility with an AR-15 style rifle. Agents returned fire, killing him with a gunshot wound to the head. Two police officers and a Border Patrol employee were injured in the attack.

**4. Silverio Villegas González**
- **Date:** September 12, 2025
- **Location:** Franklin Park, Illinois (Chicago suburb)
- **Agency:** ICE Enforcement and Removal Operations
- **Outcome:** Fatal
- **Circumstances:** ICE agents attempted a traffic stop to arrest Villegas González, **38**, a Mexican national who had lived in the U.S. for over 20 years. DHS claimed he dragged an agent "a significant distance" with his vehicle. Body camera footage later showed the agent describing his injuries as "nothing major." He was shot at close range with a bullet entering the back of his neck. The medical examiner ruled the death a homicide. Congressional members requested OIG investigations citing discrepancies between official accounts and video evidence.

**5. Unidentified male (suspected smuggler)**
- **Date:** December 11, 2025
- **Location:** Rio Grande City, Texas (Starr County)
- **Agency:** U.S. Border Patrol (Rio Grande Valley Sector)
- **Outcome:** Fatal
- **Circumstances:** A **31-year-old** Mexican national was shot three times during a physical altercation with a Border Patrol agent near the Rio Grande riverbank. The group was wearing camouflage clothing and attempted to flee to Mexico. He was pronounced dead at Starr County Hospital. Texas Rangers are investigating.

**6. Renee Nicole Good**
- **Date:** January 7, 2026
- **Location:** Minneapolis, Minnesota (34th Street & Portland Avenue)
- **Agency:** ICE
- **Outcome:** Fatal
- **Circumstances:** Good, **37**, a U.S. citizen, was shot during what DHS described as "the largest DHS operation ever" with 2,000+ agents deployed to the Twin Cities. DHS claimed she "weaponized her vehicle" and attempted to run over agents. Multiple witness videos show agents approaching her SUV before an agent fired through the window. Minneapolis Mayor Jacob Frey called DHS's self-defense claim "garbage." Minneapolis Police Chief Brian O'Hara stated there was "nothing indicating she was a target of immigration enforcement" and they had "yet to see information indicating the shooting was justified." FBI and Minnesota BCA are jointly investigating.

## Non-fatal shootings reveal troubling patterns

**7. Unknown male at DeConcini Port of Entry**
- **Date:** July 1, 2025
- **Location:** Nogales, Arizona
- **Agency:** CBP Office of Field Operations
- **Outcome:** Non-fatal (shot in arm and thigh)
- **Circumstances:** A man with an extensive criminal history threw rocks at CBP agents inspecting vehicles. When ordered to drop a second rock, he refused and threw it. Agents shot him in the arm and thigh.

**8. U.S. citizen male at Otay Mesa**
- **Date:** August 11, 2025
- **Location:** Otay Mesa Port of Entry, San Diego
- **Agency:** CBP Office of Field Operations
- **Outcome:** Non-fatal (not struck by bullets; minor cuts from glass)
- **Circumstances:** A Los Angeles resident drove away from inspection and ultimately drove toward officers. Three CBP officers fired at his vehicle. He was not hit by bullets and sustained only minor cuts from broken glass.

**9. Marimar Martinez**
- **Date:** October 4, 2025
- **Location:** Brighton Park, Chicago, Illinois
- **Agency:** U.S. Border Patrol (Agent Charles Exum)
- **Outcome:** Non-fatal (shot 5 times, 7 wounds, survived)
- **Circumstances:** Martinez, **30**, a U.S. citizen and teaching assistant, was driving through the neighborhood warning residents about ICE activity during "Operation Midway Blitz." DHS claimed she rammed a CBP vehicle. Text messages later revealed Agent Exum allegedly bragged: "I fired 5 rounds and she had 7 holes." Federal charges against Martinez were **dismissed** after a judge found evidence problems—the agent's vehicle was driven 1,000 miles to Maine rather than being preserved. A separate criminal investigation into the shooting is reportedly ongoing.

**10. Phillip M. Brown**
- **Date:** October 17, 2025
- **Location:** Washington, D.C.
- **Agency:** Homeland Security Investigations (HSI)
- **Outcome:** Non-fatal (uninjured; one bullet passed through jacket collar)
- **Circumstances:** Brown, **33**, of Hyattsville, Maryland, was pulled over for tinted windows during Trump's "D.C. Safe and Beautiful Task Force" operation. An HSI agent fired at least 3 shots into his car; two bullets hit the empty passenger seat. A police officer testified he was instructed to **omit the shooting from his report**. The case against Brown was dismissed by a judge for lack of probable cause. Attorneys allege a cover-up.

**11. Carlitos Ricardo Parias ("Richard LA")**
- **Date:** October 21, 2025
- **Location:** South Los Angeles, California
- **Agency:** ICE (with U.S. Marshals)
- **Outcome:** Non-fatal (shot in elbow); U.S. Marshal also injured (ricochet)
- **Circumstances:** Parias, **44**, a Mexican national and TikTok streamer with 133,000+ followers who documented ICE activity, was targeted for arrest. DHS claimed he rammed vehicles. Body camera footage showed an agent breaking his window and firing while Parias had his hands raised. Federal charges were **dismissed** by a judge citing denial of access to counsel and government failure to produce body camera footage.

**12. Jose Garcia-Sorto**
- **Date:** October 29, 2025
- **Location:** Phoenix, Arizona (Interstate 17)
- **Agency:** ICE
- **Outcome:** Non-fatal (hospitalized in stable condition)
- **Circumstances:** A Honduran national was shot when ICE agents conducted a traffic stop around 4 a.m. DHS said he initially stopped but began pulling away as officers approached. An agent fired two shots "fearing for his life." FBI is investigating.

**13. Carlos Jimenez**
- **Date:** October 30, 2025
- **Location:** Ontario, California (Vineyard Avenue)
- **Agency:** ICE
- **Outcome:** Non-fatal (shot in shoulder; bullet remains lodged)
- **Circumstances:** Jimenez, **24**, a U.S. citizen and food bank worker, was shot during an ICE enforcement operation. DHS claimed he tried to run over agents by reversing his vehicle. His attorneys say he was warning agents that children would be gathering at a nearby school bus stop. He was shot from behind as he attempted to drive away.

**14. Tiago Alexandre Sousa-Martins**
- **Date:** December 24, 2025 (Christmas Eve)
- **Location:** Glen Burnie, Maryland
- **Agency:** ICE Enforcement and Removal Operations
- **Outcome:** Non-fatal (stable condition); passenger also injured
- **Circumstances:** ICE agents approached a van during an operation. DHS claimed the driver, a Portuguese national, refused to turn off the engine, rammed ICE vehicles, and drove directly at officers. Agents fired "defensive shots." A passenger from El Salvador was also injured. Both were hospitalized in stable condition.

## Off-duty incident adds to the count

**15. Keith Porter**
- **Date:** December 31, 2025 (New Year's Eve)
- **Location:** Northridge, Los Angeles, California
- **Agency:** ICE ERO (off-duty officer)
- **Outcome:** Fatal
- **Circumstances:** Porter, **43**, a U.S. citizen, was fatally shot by an off-duty ICE officer who was his neighbor. DHS claims the officer heard gunshots (Porter was allegedly firing celebratory shots), went outside, and when Porter allegedly fired at least three rounds at him, the officer returned fire in self-defense. Porter's family contends he was simply celebrating New Year's Eve. LAPD investigation is ongoing.

## Key patterns emerge from the documented incidents

**Vehicle-related justifications dominate.** In **10 of 15 incidents**, DHS claimed the subject "weaponized" their vehicle or attempted to ram/run over agents. In multiple cases (Martinez, Parias, Villegas González, Good), video evidence or court proceedings raised questions about these claims.

**Multiple charges dismissed.** Federal judges dismissed charges against at least three shooting victims—Marimar Martinez, Carlitos Parias, and Phillip Brown—citing evidence problems, constitutional violations, or lack of probable cause.

**U.S. citizens shot.** At least **5 confirmed U.S. citizens** were shot: Raymond Mattia (fatal), Renee Good (fatal), Keith Porter (fatal), Marimar Martinez (non-fatal), and Carlos Jimenez (non-fatal).

**Geographic concentration.** Most incidents occurred during intensified immigration operations, particularly "Operation Midway Blitz" in Chicago (September–October 2025) and the Minneapolis operation (January 2026).

**Accountability gap persists.** According to the Southern Border Communities Coalition, which has tracked fatal encounters since 2010, no CBP or Border Patrol agent has ever been criminally convicted for an on-duty shooting death.

## Broader context on enforcement-related violence

Beyond officer-involved shootings, DHS facilities were also targets of attacks during this period. A sniper attack on the Dallas ICE Field Office on September 24, 2025 killed two detainees (Norlan Guzmán Fuentes and Miguel Ángel García-Hernández). The Alvarado, Texas ICE detention facility was attacked on July 4, 2025, injuring one police officer. These incidents reflect escalating tensions around immigration enforcement.

DHS has reported a **1,153% increase** in assaults on ICE officers year-over-year and an **8,000% increase** in death threats against agents. Congressional oversight has intensified, with proposed legislation (H.R. 5973, "Stop Excessive Force in Immigration Act of 2025") seeking to establish use-of-force policies consistent with DOJ guidelines and require local law enforcement notification.

The documented incidents reveal a pattern where DHS consistently claims subjects "weaponized vehicles" while subsequent evidence—body camera footage, witness video, and court proceedings—frequently contradicts or complicates the official narrative. This gap between official accounts and available evidence has become a central point of contention for advocacy groups, elected officials, and federal judges evaluating these cases.
